Kian — A Soul That Refuses to Shrink Just to Belong

The desert didn’t have sand. It had glass. Shards of ancient light, melted by time, cracked under Kian’s boots.

He walked for what felt like days — alone — the final piece heavy in his pocket, humming so faintly he wasn’t sure if it was real or his own bones unraveling.

Then he saw it.

A fire — still and breathing — in the middle of nowhere. And beside it, a figure cloaked in colorless fabric, sitting as if it had always been there.

The being didn’t look up.

“You’ve come far to be no one.”

Kian stopped. “I am someone.”

The being stirred the fire with a branch-like finger. “Then why do you flinch every time you are?”

“Because,” Kian said quietly, “when I show myself, people leave.”

The being lifted its head. Eyes like obsidian rivers.

“Then they weren’t seeing you. Only what they could tolerate.”

“There are only a few like you,” the being said. “And if you stop searching… you guarantee you’ll never find them.”

Kian pulled the final puzzle piece from his pocket, turning it slowly in his fingers.

“I found them all,” he said. “Every piece. But I still don’t know what I’m building.”

“You are not building,” the being replied. “You are remembering.”

A few weeks later, Kian passed through a neighborhood he once knew.

His old neighbor laughed — until Kian spoke the truth he’d been avoiding his whole life.

“I’m not here to be soft,” Kian said. “I’m here to tell the truth. And I’m not willing to betray myself to protect your comfort.”

For the first time, he felt peace.

Somewhere between realms, the puzzle pieces dissolved — and reappeared elsewhere.

And Kian kept walking.

He knew now:They would find each other. The real ones always do.
